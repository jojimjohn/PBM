How to Create a Supplier Contract

This step-by-step guide walks you through creating supplier contracts with location-specific material rates. Contracts define your agreements with suppliers: which locations, what materials, and at what rates.

Required Role & Estimated Time

Manager or Administrator | ~6 minutes to complete

Why Contracts Matter

Rate Automation

Contract rates auto-apply during collections, eliminating manual pricing and errors.

Location-Specific Pricing

Different rates for different supplier locations to reflect logistics costs.

Price Protection

Lock in favorable rates that protect against market fluctuations.

Audit Trail

Complete record of agreed terms for compliance and dispute resolution.

Before You Begin

Prerequisites
  • The supplier must exist in the system (Supplier Management)
  • Supplier locations must be configured
  • Materials to be contracted must be defined

Step 1: Navigate to Contracts Module

  1. Go to Contracts

    Navigate to: Contracts from the main menu (or Purchase ‚Üí Contracts tab)

    The contracts list shows all existing contracts with their status, supplier, and validity period.

  2. Click "New Contract"

    Click the + New Contract button.

    The contract form modal opens.

Step 2: Select Supplier

  1. Choose Supplier from Dropdown

    Select the supplier for this contract from the Supplier dropdown.

    Once selected, the supplier's locations are automatically loaded for rate configuration.

    Multiple Contracts

    You can have multiple contracts with the same supplier for different time periods or locations. Only one can be "Active" per supplier at a time.

  2. Set Contract Number (Optional)

    Enter a contract reference number for your records. This could match your paper contract number.

Step 3: Set Contract Period

  1. Set Start Date

    Select when the contract becomes effective.

    Rates only apply to collections on or after this date.

  2. Set End Date

    Select when the contract expires.

    The system will alert you before expiry so you can negotiate renewal.

    Expiry Alerts

    You'll see notifications 30, 14, and 7 days before contract expiry. Plan renewals in advance.

  3. Select Contract Type
    Type Description
    Standard Regular supplier contract with standard terms
    Exclusive Exclusive rights to collect from this supplier
    Spot Short-term or one-time arrangement

Step 4: Configure Location Material Rates

This is the core of the contract - defining what you'll pay for each material at each location.

  1. Select a Location

    In the Location Material Rates section, you'll see the supplier's locations listed.

    Click on a location to expand it and add material rates.

  2. Add Material Rate

    Click + Add Material within a location:

    Field Description
    Material Select the material (e.g., Engine Oil, Transformer Oil)
    Rate Type How the rate is calculated (see Rate Types below)
    Rate/Value The rate amount or percentage
    Min Quantity Minimum collection quantity (optional)
    Max Quantity Maximum collection quantity (optional)
  3. Understanding Rate Types

    Fixed Rate

    Definition: A locked price per unit until contract expiry.

    Use when: You want price stability regardless of market changes.

    Example: OMR 24.00 per liter for Engine Oil

    Discount Percentage

    Definition: A percentage discount off the current market price.

    Use when: You want to benefit from market price drops.

    Example: 10% discount off market price

    Minimum Price Guarantee

    Definition: You pay the lower of contract rate OR market rate.

    Use when: You want maximum flexibility and best price.

    Example: Max OMR 25.00 or market, whichever is less

    Free / We Pay

    Free: Supplier gives materials at no charge (disposal fee waived).

    We Pay: You charge the supplier (e.g., hazardous waste disposal).

  4. Add Rates for Multiple Materials

    Repeat the process to add rates for all materials you'll collect from this location.

    Different locations can have different rates for the same material.

  5. Configure Additional Locations

    If the supplier has multiple collection points, configure rates for each location.

    Location-Based Pricing

    Remote locations might have different rates due to transportation costs. Configure each location appropriately.

Step 5: Add Contract Terms (Optional)

  1. Payment Terms

    Specify payment terms: Net 30, Net 60, Cash on Collection, etc.

  2. Special Conditions

    Add any special conditions or notes in the terms field:

    • Minimum collection frequency
    • Quality requirements
    • Exclusivity clauses
    • Penalty terms

Step 6: Save and Activate Contract

  1. Click "Save Contract"

    Click Save Contract to create the contract.

    The contract is created in Draft status.

  2. Review the Contract

    Open the contract to review all details:

    • Supplier information
    • Contract period
    • All location-material rates
    • Terms and conditions
  3. Activate the Contract

    When ready to use the contract:

    1. Open the contract
    2. Click Change Status
    3. Select Active
    4. Confirm the activation
    One Active Contract

    Only one contract per supplier can be active. Activating a new contract will prompt you about the existing active contract.

Troubleshooting

Issue Solution
Supplier not in dropdown Create supplier first in Supplier Management
No locations showing Add locations to the supplier in Supplier Locations
Material not in list Material must be created in Materials Management
Can't activate contract Check that start date is today or future, and another contract isn't already active
Rates not applying to collections Ensure contract is "Active" and collection date is within contract period
