How to Create a Sales Order

This step-by-step guide walks you through creating a sales order with automatic contract rate application and FIFO inventory tracking. Learn how to select customers, add materials, preview profit margins, and manage orders through their complete lifecycle.

Required Role & Estimated Time

Sales Staff or higher | ~5 minutes to complete

Step 1: Navigate to Sales Module

  1. Go to Sales

    Navigate to: Sales from the main menu

    The Sales Orders tab shows all your existing orders. You can view, track, and manage orders from here.

  2. Click "New Sales Order"

    Click the + New Sales Order button in the top right area.

    A modal form opens for creating the new order.

Step 2: Select Customer

  1. Choose Customer from Dropdown

    Select the customer for this order from the Customer dropdown.

    Contract Status Indicator

    Customers with active contracts show a ‚óè green indicator. Their negotiated rates will automatically apply.

  2. Customer Not Found?

    If the customer doesn't exist:

    1. Go to Sales ‚Üí Customers
    2. Click + Add Customer
    3. Fill in customer details and save
    4. Return to create the sales order

Step 3: Add Materials to Order

  1. Click "Add Item"

    In the materials section, click + Add Item to add a material to the order.

  2. Select Material

    Choose the material from the dropdown. The system immediately checks for contract rates.

    Field Description
    Material Select the material being sold
    Quantity Enter the quantity to sell
    Unit Auto-filled based on material (liters, kg, etc.)
    Rate Auto-filled from contract or enter market rate
  3. Understanding Contract Rate Display

    When a material is added, the rate display shows:

    Contract Rate Applied ‚úì

    Green highlight means a contract rate is in effect.

    Example:
    Rate: OMR 24.00/liter (Contract)
    Savings: OMR 2.00/liter vs market

    Market Rate

    No contract or contract doesn't cover this material.

    Example:
    Rate: OMR 26.00/liter (Market)
    Standard pricing applied

  4. Contract Rate Types Explained
    Rate Type How It Works
    Fixed Rate Customer pays the negotiated rate regardless of market changes. Protects against price increases.
    Discount % A percentage discount off the current market price. Rate fluctuates with market.
    Minimum Price Guarantee Customer pays the lower of contract rate OR market rate. Best deal for customer.
  5. Add More Items (Optional)

    Click + Add Item again to add more materials to the same order.

    The subtotal updates automatically as you add items.

Step 4: Preview FIFO Allocation (Optional)

What is FIFO?

FIFO (First-In, First-Out) ensures older inventory is sold first. This helps with accurate cost tracking, proper inventory rotation, and precise profit calculations.

  1. Click "Preview FIFO"

    After adding materials, click the Preview FIFO button to see inventory allocation details.

    This is optional but recommended for larger orders or when you need to verify profit margins.

  2. Review Batch Allocations

    The FIFO Preview modal shows which inventory batches will be used:

    Column Description
    Batch Number Unique identifier for each inventory batch
    Purchase Date When the batch was purchased (oldest used first)
    Available Qty Quantity remaining in the batch
    Allocated Qty Quantity that will be used for this order
    Unit Cost Original purchase cost per unit
    Total Cost Cost for this allocation (Qty √ó Unit Cost)
  3. Review Summary & Profit Analysis

    At the bottom of the FIFO preview, review the profit summary:

    Sales Revenue

    Total selling price based on contract or market rates.

    COGS (Cost of Goods Sold)

    Total cost from FIFO batch allocations.

    Gross Margin

    Revenue minus COGS with profit percentage.

  4. Check Stock Warnings

    The preview shows warnings if:

    Insufficient Stock

    Order quantity exceeds available inventory. Reduce quantity or wait for stock replenishment.

  5. Close Preview

    Click Close to return to the order form. The preview doesn't commit any changes yet.

Step 5: Review Your Order

  1. Check Items Table

    Review the items table to verify:

    • All materials are correct
    • Quantities are accurate
    • Rates show contract pricing where applicable
    • Subtotals are correct

    You can edit or remove items using the action buttons.

  2. Contract Warnings

    Watch for warning indicators:

    Contract Expiring Soon

    Yellow warning means the contract expires soon. Consider discussing renewal with the customer.

    Contract Expired

    Red warning means the contract has expired. Market rates will apply.

  3. Add Notes (Optional)

    Use the notes field for:

    • Delivery instructions
    • Special handling requirements
    • Customer reference numbers

Step 6: Create the Sales Order

  1. Click "Create Sales Order"

    Once you've reviewed everything, click Create Sales Order.

    The order is created in Draft status.

  2. Order Confirmation

    You'll see a success message with the order number (e.g., SO-1234567890).

    The new order appears in your Sales Orders table ready for processing.

Quick Actions

Use the quick action buttons in the Sales Orders table to progress orders without opening them. Hover over an order row to see available actions.

Troubleshooting

Issue Solution
Customer not in dropdown Create the customer first in Sales ‚Üí Customers
Contract rate not applying Check if contract is active and covers this material
Material not showing Ensure material is active and has inventory
Wrong rate displayed Review contract terms - may have different rate type
Can't create order Check that all required fields are filled
FIFO preview shows insufficient stock Order quantity exceeds available inventory. Reduce quantity or wait for stock replenishment through purchases.
Can't delete order Only Draft and Cancelled orders can be deleted. Cancel the order first if needed.
Inventory not updating after delivery Check browser console for errors. The backend server may need restart if code was recently updated.
COGS showing as zero Verify inventory batches exist with purchase costs. COGS is calculated from actual batch costs.
