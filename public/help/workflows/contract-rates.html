Using Contract Rates

Learn how contract rates work in sales orders. This guide explains automatic rate application, understanding rate indicators, and handling expired contracts.

Who Uses This

Sales Staff creating sales orders for customers with contracts.

Understanding Contract Rates

Contract rates are special prices negotiated with customers. When active, they override market prices.

Rate Types

Type Description Example
Fixed Rate Set price regardless of market OMR 24.00/L (even if market is OMR 28/L)
Discount % Percentage off market price 10% off → OMR 25.20/L when market is OMR 28/L
Minimum Guarantee Customer pays lower of contract or market Max OMR 26/L - pays less if market drops

How Contract Rates Apply in Sales Orders

  1. Select Customer

    When you select a customer, the system checks for active contracts.

  2. Select Delivery Location

    Contract rates are location-specific. Selecting the delivery location determines which rates apply.

  3. Add Materials

    For each material added:

    1. System checks if material has a contract rate for this location
    2. If yes, contract rate is applied automatically
    3. If no, market rate is used
  4. Review Rate Indicators

    Look for visual indicators showing which rate is applied.

Understanding Rate Indicators

The sales order form shows colored indicators to help you understand pricing:

Green - Contract Rate Active

Contract rate is lower than market. Customer is getting a deal.

Shows: "Contract Rate: OMR 24.00 (Market: OMR 28.00)"

Yellow - Contract Expiring Soon

Contract expires within 30 days. Consider renewal discussion.

Shows: "Contract expires in X days"

Red - Contract Expired

Contract has expired. Market rate will apply.

Shows: "Contract expired on [date]"

Gray - No Contract

No contract exists for this customer/location/material. Market rate applies.

Creating an Order with Contract Rates

  1. Go to Sales Orders

    Navigate to Sales → Sales Orders and click + New Order.

  2. Select Customer

    Choose the customer. If they have an active contract, you'll see a Contract Active badge.

  3. Select Location

    Choose the delivery location. Rates are specific to each location.

  4. Add Materials

    Click + Add Item and select materials. Watch for:

    • Contract rate automatically applied
    • Comparison with market rate shown
    • Total savings calculated
  5. Review Order Total

    The order summary shows total value with contract rates applied.

  6. Complete and Submit

    Add delivery date and payment terms, then submit the order.

Handling Expired Contracts

When a customer's contract has expired:

Options

  1. Use market rate: Proceed with order at current market prices
  2. Contact customer: Discuss contract renewal before order
  3. Escalate to manager: For important customers, get guidance
Proactive Approach

Check the "Expiring Contracts" report weekly. Contact customers 30 days before expiry to discuss renewal.

Best Practices

  • Verify rates: Double-check contract rates match expectations
  • Watch for warnings: Pay attention to yellow/red indicators
  • Communicate proactively: Notify customers of expiring contracts
  • Document discussions: Note any pricing discussions with customers
  • Know your contracts: Familiarize yourself with key customer contracts
