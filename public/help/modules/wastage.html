Wastage Management

Track and manage material wastage throughout your operations. The Wastage module helps you record losses, maintain accurate inventory, and analyze wastage patterns for improvement.

Who Can Access

Purchase Staff can record wastage. Managers approve wastage. Admins have full access.

How Wastage Affects Inventory

Important: Inventory is Only Reduced When Wastage is Approved

Recording wastage does NOT immediately reduce inventory. The wastage record stays in "Pending" status until a manager approves it. Only upon approval is the inventory actually reduced. This ensures accurate tracking and prevents errors.

Understanding Wastage Types

Wastage in oil trading can occur due to various reasons:

Spillage

Accidental spills during collection, transfer, or storage of oil products.

Evaporation

Natural loss of volatile products over time, especially in hot storage conditions.

Contamination

Materials rendered unusable due to mixing or exposure to contaminants.

Damage

Container damage leading to product loss (leaking drums, punctured tanks).

Transport Loss

Loss during transportation from supplier location to warehouse.

Quality Rejection

Materials rejected during quality inspection that cannot be used.

Recording Wastage

There are two ways to record wastage in the system:

Method 1: From Collection Details (Recommended)

Use this method when wastage occurred during a specific collection.

  1. Open Collection Details

    In the Collections module, click on a finalized collection order to view its details.

  2. Click "Record Wastage"

    In the Wastage section, click the orange + Record Wastage button.

    This opens a pre-filled form with the collection's materials.

  3. Select Material and Enter Details

    The form shows only materials from this collection. Select the affected material and enter:

    • Quantity: Amount of wastage
    • Unit Cost: Cost per unit for COGS calculation
    • Wastage Type: Reason for the loss
    • Description: Detailed explanation
  4. Submit

    Click Save. The wastage is linked to the collection and sent for approval.

Method 2: From Collection Orders Table

Quick access via the wastage icon in the actions column.

  1. Find the Collection

    In the Collection Orders table, locate the finalized collection order.

  2. Click the Wastage Icon

    Click the droplet icon (üíß) in the Actions column. This opens the wastage form pre-linked to that collection.

Method 3: From Wastage Module (General)

Use this for wastage not related to a specific collection.

  1. Navigate to Wastage

    Go to Inventory ‚Üí Wastage and click + Record Wastage.

  2. Fill in Details

    Select material, enter quantity, choose type, and optionally link to a collection.

Wastage Approval Process

All wastage records require manager approval before inventory is adjusted.

For Managers

  1. View Pending Approvals

    Go to Wastage ‚Üí Pending Approvals tab.

  2. Review Details

    Examine the wastage record: material, quantity, reason, linked collection, and any attachments.

  3. Approve or Reject
    • Approve: Inventory is reduced using FIFO allocation; COGS is calculated
    • Reject: Record is marked rejected; no inventory change
FIFO Inventory Allocation

When wastage is approved, the system uses FIFO (First In, First Out) to deduct from the oldest inventory batches first. This ensures accurate Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) calculations.

Amending Approved Wastage

If you need to correct an approved wastage record, you must use the Amendment feature (not regular edit).

Amendment Adjusts Inventory Automatically

When you amend an approved wastage:

  • If new quantity is higher: Additional inventory is deducted
  • If new quantity is lower: Some inventory is restored

How to Amend

  1. Find the Approved Record

    In the Wastage list, locate the approved record you need to correct.

  2. Click "Amend"

    Click the Amend button (only visible for approved records).

  3. Enter New Details

    Update the quantity and provide an amendment note explaining why the change is needed. This note is required for audit purposes.

  4. Submit

    The amendment is processed and inventory is automatically adjusted by the difference.

Wastage Analytics

Track wastage patterns to identify improvement opportunities:

Available Reports

  • Wastage by Material: See which products have highest wastage
  • Wastage by Type: Understand common causes (spillage vs evaporation)
  • Monthly Trends: Track wastage over time
  • Cost Impact: Financial impact of wastage on operations
  • By Collection: View wastage linked to specific collections

Best Practices

  • Record immediately: Log wastage as soon as it happens while details are fresh
  • Link to collections: Always link wastage to the relevant collection for better tracking
  • Be accurate: Measure quantities precisely - estimates lead to inventory discrepancies
  • Document thoroughly: Include photos or documentation when possible
  • Identify root causes: Use the wastage type to track patterns
  • Review regularly: Monthly wastage review helps identify issues early
  • Use amendments: Never create new records to "correct" approved ones - use the Amendment feature
