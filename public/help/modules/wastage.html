Wastage Management

Track and manage material wastage throughout your operations. The Wastage module helps you record losses, maintain accurate inventory, and analyze wastage patterns for improvement.

Who Can Access

Purchase Staff can record wastage. Managers approve wastage. Admins have full access.

Understanding Wastage

Wastage in oil trading can occur due to various reasons:

Spillage

Accidental spills during collection, transfer, or storage of oil products.

Evaporation

Natural loss of volatile products over time, especially in hot storage conditions.

Contamination

Materials rendered unusable due to mixing or exposure to contaminants.

Damage

Container damage leading to product loss (leaking drums, punctured tanks).

Recording Wastage

  1. Navigate to Wastage

    Go to Inventory → Wastage and click + Record Wastage.

  2. Select Material

    Choose the material that was wasted from the dropdown.

  3. Enter Quantity

    Enter the exact quantity lost. The system will show the value based on cost price.

  4. Select Wastage Type

    Choose the reason for wastage:

    • Spillage
    • Evaporation
    • Contamination
    • Damage
    • Expired
    • Other (specify)
  5. Add Details
    • Date: When the wastage occurred
    • Location: Where it happened
    • Description: Detailed explanation of what happened
  6. Submit for Approval

    Click Submit. The wastage record goes to manager for approval before affecting inventory.

Wastage Approval Process

All wastage records require manager approval before inventory is adjusted.

For Managers

  1. View Pending Approvals

    Go to Wastage → Pending Approvals tab.

  2. Review Details

    Examine the wastage record: material, quantity, reason, and supporting documentation.

  3. Approve or Reject
    • Approve: Inventory is deducted; wastage is recorded in reports
    • Reject: Provide reason; record is sent back for correction
Important

Approved wastage permanently reduces inventory. Ensure the record is accurate before approving.

Wastage Record Statuses

Status Description
Pending Awaiting manager approval
Approved Approved and deducted from inventory
Rejected Rejected with reason - needs correction or removal

Wastage Analytics

Track wastage patterns to identify improvement opportunities:

Available Reports

  • Wastage by Material: See which products have highest wastage
  • Wastage by Type: Understand common causes (spillage vs evaporation)
  • Monthly Trends: Track wastage over time
  • Cost Impact: Financial impact of wastage on operations
Use Analytics

Regular review of wastage analytics can help identify handling issues, storage problems, or training needs.

Best Practices

  • Record immediately: Log wastage as soon as it happens while details are fresh
  • Be accurate: Measure quantities precisely - estimates lead to inventory discrepancies
  • Document thoroughly: Include photos or documentation when possible
  • Identify root causes: Use the wastage type to track patterns
  • Review regularly: Monthly wastage review helps identify issues early
  • Train staff: Proper handling training reduces spillage and damage
